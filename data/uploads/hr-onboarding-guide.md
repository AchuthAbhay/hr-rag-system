## 1. Pre-Joining Preparation

### 1.1 Documents Required on Day 1
Please bring the following documents on your first day:

**Identity Proof (any one):**
- PAN Card (mandatory for salary processing)
- Aadhaar Card
- Passport
- Driving License

**Address Proof (any one):**
- Aadhaar Card
- Utility bill (electricity/water - not older than 3 months)
- Bank statement with address
- Rental agreement

**Educational Documents:**
- All degree certificates (10th, 12th, Graduation, Post-graduation)
- Mark sheets for all semesters
- Provisional certificate if final degree pending

- Relieving letters from previous employers

### 1.2 Pre-Joining Formalities
You will receive an email 5 days before your joining date with:
- Joining date confirmation
- Reporting time: 10:00 AM
- Reporting location: HR Reception, 3rd Floor, Building A
- Your onboarding buddy's contact information
- Dress code: Business casual for first day
- Parking information (if applicable)

### 1.3 Background Verification
Your background verification is conducted by a third-party agency and includes:
- Education verification (15-20 days)
- Employment verification (10-15 days)
- Criminal record check (10-12 days)
- Address verification (7-10 days)

Your joining is provisional until background verification is complete. Any discrepancies may result in termination of employment.

## 4. Probation Period (First 6 Months)

### 4.1 What to Expect
Your first 6 months are a probationary period designed for mutual evaluation. During this time:

**Month 1-2: Learning & Orientation**
- Understand company culture, processes, and tools
- Shadow senior team members
- Work on small, guided tasks
- Ask questions - lots of them!

**Month 3: Mid-Probation Review**
- Formal review meeting with your manager
- Feedback on performance, cultural fit, and skills
- Discussion of strengths and areas for improvement
- Goal adjustment if needed

**Month 4-5: Independent Contribution**
- Take ownership of tasks/projects
- Demonstrate technical/functional skills
- Build relationships across teams
- Show initiative and problem-solving ability

**Month 6: Probation Completion Review**
- Comprehensive performance assessment
- 360-degree feedback from peers and stakeholders
- Decision on confirmation of employment
- Discussion of career path and growth opportunities

### 4.2 Performance Expectations
To successfully complete probation, you should:

**Technical/Functional:**
- Demonstrate competency in core skills for your role
- Complete assigned tasks with minimal supervision by Month 5
- Show continuous learning and skill improvement

**Cultural Fit:**
- Align with TechVista values (Innovation, Integrity, Collaboration, Excellence)
- Actively participate in team activities
- Communicate effectively and transparently
- Adapt to company processes and work culture

**Attitude & Initiative:**
- Proactive in seeking help when stuck
- Open to feedback and willing to improve
- Take ownership of deliverables
- Show enthusiasm and commitment

### 4.3 Probation Extension or Termination
If performance is not meeting expectations:
- **Extension:** Probation may be extended by up to 3 months with clear improvement goals
- **Termination:** Employment may be terminated with 1 week notice or pay in lieu

Probation extensions or terminations are communicated formally by HR after discussion with your manager.

## 5. Work Culture & Values

### 5.1 TechVista Core Values

**Innovation:**
- We encourage experimentation and creative problem-solving
- Failure is a learning opportunity, not a career risk
- Innovation hours: 4 hours per month for personal learning/side projects

**Integrity:**
- Honesty in all communications (even when delivering bad news)
- Ethical decision-making in client and vendor interactions
- Transparent feedback culture

**Collaboration:**
- Cross-functional teamwork is the norm
- Open-door policy - leadership is approachable
- Knowledge sharing through internal tech talks and brown bag sessions

**Excellence:**
- High-quality work is non-negotiable
- Continuous improvement mindset
- Taking pride in deliverables

### 5.2 Communication Norms

**Email:**
- Use for formal communications, documentation, and external stakeholders
- Response expected within 24 hours during work days
- Use clear subject lines and keep messages concise

**Slack:**
- Primary tool for internal quick communication
- @channel and @here to be used sparingly (urgent matters only)
- Use threads to keep conversations organized
- Status updates: Set your status when away/in meetings/focused work

**Meetings:**
- All meetings must have agenda shared 24 hours in advance
- Mandatory attendees should be kept minimal (5-7 people max)
- Meetings should start and end on time
- Video on for team meetings (builds connection)
- No meetings on Friday afternoons (focus time for deep work)

### 5.3 Work-Life Balance Initiatives

**Flexible Working:**
- Hybrid model: 3 days in office, 2 days remote (manager approval required for changes)
- Core hours: 10 AM - 4 PM (be available for meetings)
- No expectation to respond to emails/Slack after 7 PM or on weekends

**Wellness Programs:**
- Mental health support: Free counseling sessions (3 per year via Employee Assistance Program)
- Wellness Wednesday: 30-minute guided meditation/yoga session every Wednesday at 4 PM
- Quarterly wellness challenges with prizes
- Annual sports day and team outings

**Family Support:**
- Maternity leave: 26 weeks paid
- Paternity leave: 15 days paid
- Child care support: ‚Çπ3,000/month reimbursement for parents of children under 5 years
- Flexible hours for parents with school-going children

## 8. Career Growth & Development

### 8.1 Career Ladder
TechVista has clear career progression paths:

**Individual Contributor Track:**
- Junior Engineer/Analyst (Entry level)
- Engineer/Analyst (2-3 years)
- Senior Engineer/Analyst (4-6 years)
- Lead Engineer/Sr. Analyst (7-9 years)
- Principal Engineer/Principal Analyst (10+ years)

**Management Track:**
- Team Lead (3-5 years experience)
- Engineering Manager / Project Manager (6-8 years)
- Senior Manager (9-12 years)
- Director (12-15 years)
- VP / Head (15+ years)

You can switch between IC and management tracks with manager discussion and HR approval.

### 8.2 Annual Performance & Promotion Cycle
**January-February:** Performance reviews
- Self-assessment submission
- Manager evaluation
- 360-degree feedback from peers
- Rating: 1 (Needs Improvement) to 5 (Outstanding)

**March:** Performance discussions and rating finalization

**April:** Increments and promotions effective
- Increment range: 8-15% based on performance
- Promotions typically require 2 consecutive years of "Exceeds Expectations" (rating 4+)

### 8.3 Internal Job Mobility
- Internal job postings visible on employee portal
- Eligible to apply after 1 year in current role
- Must inform current manager before applying
- Interview process similar to external candidates
- Notice period for internal transfers: 30 days

### 8.4 Mentorship Program
- Available for employees after probation
- Mentors assigned based on career goals
- Monthly 1-on-1 sessions (30-45 minutes)
- Focus on skill development, career guidance, and networking

## 10. FAQs

**Q: When will I receive my first salary?**  
A: Salary is processed on the last working day of the month. Pro-rata salary for your joining month will be paid in the next cycle.

**Q: Can I apply for leave during probation?**  
A: Yes, but it's recommended to avoid long leaves in the first 3 months. Emergency leaves are always allowed.

**Q: What if I need to resign during probation?**  
A: Notice period is 30 days during probation. You can also opt for pay-in-lieu with mutual agreement.

**Q: Can I work from home full-time?**  
A: Fully remote work requires VP approval and is granted only in exceptional cases (relocation, medical reasons, etc.).

**Q: How do I enroll my family for health insurance?**  
A: Complete the enrollment form in Darwinbox within 30 days of joining. Late enrollment will be allowed only during the annual renewal window.

**Q: Are there any dress code restrictions?**  
A: Business casual is the norm. Formals required for client meetings. Fridays are smart casual.

**Q: Can I freelance or work part-time for other companies?**  
A: Prior written approval from HR required. Typically not allowed if it's a competing business or conflicts with work hours.
